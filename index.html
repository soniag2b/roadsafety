Street Safety in New York City at the end of 2022
Killed on the Roads have not Decreased

Vision Zero's goal has not been reached

December 7th, 2022
By Sonia de Beaufort

As we are approaching the end of the year, there are no signs of decrease in the number of people killed in the streets of New York City.

At the country level, we see that some States show a strong increase like Kansas and others show a strong decrease like the District of Colombia. At the State level, New York State shows a decrease of more than 23% in ten years. However, this decrease is not reflected in New York City.

Reached by phone, Jacob deCastro, communications manager at Transportation Alternatives, recognizes that there have been improvements in New York City with a bill passed for a five-year plan and budget to improve the streets, with the speed safety cameras program that has doubled in scope, with the push for a new state legislation, Sammy's law, for a 20-mile per hour speed limit.

DeCastro says that "the best way to keep people safe is to redesign roads, extend sidewalks, and take space from cars. These actions are important to achieve Vision Zero and avoid future casualties."
